# Journey Mapping Framework
*Purpose: Journey mapping methodology with 5 moment types, 4 tactics, and the Peak-End Rule.*

A framework for identifying and optimizing the moments that matter in customer experiences.

---

## What is Journey Mapping?

Journey mapping identifies the key moments in a customer experience and applies psychology to optimize them. Unlike exhaustive journey maps, this approach focuses on **5-6 critical moments** that truly matter (Miller's Law: 7±2).

**Key insight:** Your brain doesn't average experiences—it heavily weighs specific moments.

---

## The 5 Types of Journey Moments

| Type | Symbol | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Peak** | 🏔️ | Highest absolute Psych level (best moment) |
| **Pit** | 🕳️ | Lowest absolute Psych level (worst moment) |
| **Jump** | 🌈 | Psych increases significantly |
| **Drop** | 💧 | Psych decreases significantly |
| **Transition** | 🚪 | Start or end of a major milestone |

---

## The Peak-End Rule

Research shows memories are formed primarily from:
1. **The Peak** — The most intense moment (positive or negative)
2. **The Pit** — The worst moment
3. **The End** — The final impression
4. **Transitions** — Milestone markers

**Implication:** Optimize these moments specifically rather than trying to improve everything equally.

---

## 4 Proven Tactics to Improve Journeys

### 1. Mark Transitions 🚪

Celebrate key milestones clearly. Transitions are memory anchors.

**Examples:**
- "You've completed your first week!"
- "Your plan is now active"
- "Order confirmed—here's what's next"

### 2. Elevate Peaks 🏔️

Make the best moments even better. Don't settle for "good enough."

**Questions:**
- What creates your highest peak?
- How can you amplify that moment?
- Can you add a delighter?

### 3. Fill Biggest Pits 🕳️

Fix the worst experience first. Pits damage memory disproportionately.

**Questions:**
- What causes your deepest pit?
- Can you eliminate this step entirely?
- If not, how can you add support or value here?

### 4. Reorder Steps 🔄

Sequence matters. Consider:
- Moving friction earlier (when motivation is high)
- Saving positive moments for the end
- Breaking large tasks into milestone chunks

---

## Design Principle: Clarify Complexity

Reduce cognitive load by showing what matters now. If it doesn't help a patient understand their plan or take the next step, it doesn't get shipped.

**Application to journeys:**
- Each journey moment should have one clear focus
- Remove steps that don't serve the user
- Simplify decision points within each moment

---

## The ROI of Delight

Research shows you earn **9x more revenue** by:
- Delighting "good" customers (NPS 5-8 → 9-10)
- Rather than satisfying "average" ones (NPS 0-4 → 5-8)

**Implication:** Invest in peaks, not just pit-filling.

---

## Hyperbolic Discounting

People prefer immediate small rewards over larger future ones.

**Design implications:**
- Deliver value preview early
- Break big rewards into smaller milestones
- Show progress constantly

## Journey Mapping Process

### Step 1: Create a 6P Story

Start with empathy. Your 6P Story reveals the emotional journey:
- Panel 1: Exposition (context)
- Panels 2-5: Conflict and journey
- Panel 6: Resolution

### Step 2: Identify Key Moments

From your story, extract 5-6 key moments:
1. What's the biggest emotional high?
2. What's the biggest emotional low?
3. Where does motivation spike or drop?
4. What are the major transitions?

### Step 3: Map and Score

For each moment:
- Plot emotional level (high/medium/low)
- Identify moment type (Peak/Pit/Jump/Drop/Transition)
- Calculate Psych score if helpful

### Step 4: Prioritize Improvements

Use the 4 tactics:
1. Which transitions need clearer markers?
2. How can you elevate the peak?
3. What's causing the deepest pit?
4. Should any steps be reordered?

## Common Journey Patterns

### Onboarding Journey
```
Hope (+5) → Confusion (-3) → Progress (+2) → Friction (-5) → Success (+10)
```
**Fix:** Reduce friction, add progress indicators

### Purchase Journey
```
Interest (+3) → Price Shock (-10) → Comparison (+2) → Trust (-5) → Convert (+5)
```
**Fix:** Reframe pricing, build trust earlier

### Re-engagement Journey
```
Reminder (0) → Guilt (-3) → Value preview (+4) → Return (+5)
```
**Fix:** Lead with value, avoid guilt-inducing language

## Journey Analysis Questions

### Diagnose Current State
- What's causing your biggest Drop?
- What's driving your biggest Jump?
- Where is your deepest Pit?
- What creates your highest Peak?
- How clear are your Transitions?

### Design Improvements
- Can you reorder steps for better flow?
- Can you preview value earlier?
- Can you split complex tasks?
- Can you add progress indicators?
- Can you celebrate milestones better?

---

## Emergency Fixes by Symptom

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Quick Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| High drop-off | Low ability | Simplify or split steps |
| Low engagement | Missing prompts | Add contextual triggers |
| Poor completion | Unclear value | Highlight benefits early |
| User confusion | Poor interpretation | Use familiar patterns |
| Abandonment | Psych depletion | Reduce friction, add motivation |

## Measurement: Journey Health Metrics

### Leading Indicators
- Step completion rates
- Time between steps
- Psych scores by moment
- Emotional feedback

### Lagging Indicators
- End-to-end conversion
- Customer lifetime value
- NPS by journey segment
- Support ticket volume

### Behavioral KPIs
- **Activation Rate:** % completing key behavior
- **Time to Value:** Speed to first success
- **Completion Depth:** Progress through journey
- **Behavioral Retention:** Sustained engagement
