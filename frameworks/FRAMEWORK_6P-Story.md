# 6P Story Framework
*Purpose: 6P Story structure for creating memorable, stakeholder-ready design narratives.*

A methodology for building customer empathy through visual narratives.

---

## What is the 6P Story Framework?

The 6P Story Framework is a storytelling methodology that captures complete customer journeys in six sequential panels. Instead of focusing on screens and features, 6P Stories reveal the *why* behind user behaviors through emotional, visual narratives.

**Key insight:** Stories are 22 times more memorable than standalone facts.

---

## Why Use Stories Instead of Screens?

| Approach | What it tells you |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Screens** | *What* happens |
| **User journeys** | *Where* it happens |
| **Stories** | *Why* it happens |

> "The 'real' story of your customers typically happens outside of your app."

### Common Pitfalls of Screen-Focused Design

1. **Law of the Instrument** — We focus on screens because that's what we're used to seeing
2. **Sunk Cost Fallacy** — Teams become biased toward existing solutions they've invested in
3. **Missing Context** — Screens show product interactions but miss the larger customer context

## The Psychology Behind Storytelling

### Why Stories Work

**Narrative Bias**
- Humans are evolutionarily wired to make sense of the world through stories
- Stories make information easier to process and recall
- Stories are 22x more memorable than standalone facts

**Singularity Effect**
- People empathize more with single, identifiable individuals than abstract groups
- Vivid characters are more memorable than statistics
- Personal stories create stronger emotional connections

**Character Identification Effect**
- Stories activate mirror neurons, making your brain imagine you're experiencing the journey
- This develops higher-level empathy for the story's hero
- Stories are the most effective vehicle for transferring ideas between people

### Why Comics Work for Stories

**Closure** — Panel gaps act as open-ended questions, forcing participation and sparking creativity

**Miller's Law** — Working memory holds 7±2 items. Six panels are optimal for quick comprehension while leaving room for imagination.

**Pareidolia** — Humans interpret faces and emotions in abstract shapes. Even simple stick figures build empathy.

## How to Create a 6P Story

### The Six-Panel Structure

| Panel | Purpose | Content |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **1** | Exposition | Setup and context — who is the character and what's their situation? |
| **2-5** | Conflict & Journey | Struggles, doubts, obstacles, and actions |
| **6** | Denouement | Resolution — how does the journey end? |

### Best Practices Checklist

- [ ] **Start with empathy** — Focus on customer problems, not app features
- [ ] **Capture emotions** — Show emotional ups and downs with facial expressions
- [ ] **Focus on actions** — Don't just describe steps; show what the protagonist does
- [ ] **Highlight struggles** — Panels 2-5 should showcase conflicts and obstacles (great stories focus on the struggle)
- [ ] **Look for opportunities** — Use stories to naturally highlight experience gaps
- [ ] **Keep it simple** — 7±2 elements per panel for comprehension

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Creating feature walkthroughs instead of emotional journeys
- Starting with solutions instead of problems
- Making all panels positive (no conflict = no story)
- Over-focusing on visual polish instead of emotional truth

## When to Use 6P Stories

**Perfect for:**
- Early product discovery
- Team alignment on customer problems
- Communicating user research findings
- Pitching new features or products
- Onboarding new team members

**Success indicators:**
- Team members can retell the story from memory
- Story reveals previously unknown pain points
- Solutions naturally emerge from identified gaps
- Increased team empathy for customer experience

## Quick Reference: 6P Story Template

```
Panel 1: Who is [CHARACTER] and what's their situation?
Panel 2: What problem or trigger do they encounter?
Panel 3: What do they try first? How do they feel?
Panel 4: What obstacles or doubts arise?
Panel 5: What action do they take?
Panel 6: What's the outcome? How do they feel now?
```
